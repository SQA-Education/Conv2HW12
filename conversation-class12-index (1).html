3 · Write The "Crisis Report"

Write a 7–9 sentence report to a family member or partner. Choose one of the "Crisis" scenarios from today's class. Explain what happened using the passive voice to describe the problem, and emphatic comparatives to explain why it's a big deal. Conclude with your final decision: are you moving in or walking away?

🛠️ Passive voice — focus on the process, not the person:
"The leak was discovered during the walkthrough."
"The mortgage has been approved."
"The repairs are being handled by a contractor."

📈 Emphatic comparatives: "This is significantly more stressful than we planned."

  • 7–9 sentences, written as a report/update
  • Describe the problem in the passive voice (was discovered, has been approved…)
  • Use at least one emphatic comparative
  • End with your decision: moving in or walking away?
